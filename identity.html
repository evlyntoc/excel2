TOPIC: IDENTITY AND PURPOSE


Presented by Aderonke K-Eseka

l will be as simple as I can due to the age bracket of 11-15.years, so you can understand identity in simple terms.
*What is Identity?
In simple terms it is*
- Who you are,
- The way you think about yourself,
- The way others see you
- Your characteristics: behaviour, temperament, emotions,thoughts ( how you think)
- What makes you unique from others
Furthermore, It is the qualities, beliefs, personality, looks or expression that makes of oneself ( one's mental model of oneself), self esteem and individuality.
*key facets of personal
identity*
- your name
- your age
- your sex ( biological make up I.e female, male
- your family ( family background)
- your religion
Knowing who you are and your uniqueness early in life will give you advantage to excel early in life.
It keeps you focused, confident and helps you to have a sense of purpose.

*Identity consists of 3 basic elements:*
- Personal Identity
- Family Identity
- Social Identity
*Note:*
The family background and society ( environment) where people are, are part of what makes their identity.
Going further, identity formation as described by Erik Erikson in his theory of developmental stages extends from birth through adulthood. It begins from childhood and gain prominence during adolescence.
A person's identity is formed from birth and it becomes more pronounced during adolescence and then to adulthood.
Once one can identify who they are and what they stand for, they can easily know what they want to become. This therefore lead us to what is called purpose.
We are all here on earth for a purpose and that purpose must be discovered.
*What is purpose?*
It is the reason why someone is created or why something is done.
Your purpose drives or influence your decisions, shape your goals, and offer a sense of direction.
You need to discover your purpose , what you are created to be in life. Is it a teacher, an engineer, medical doctor, etc.
